*Field Notes* began as a small PDF sent to forty-three friends. It grew, the way things do when they are made without expectations — slowly, and then all at once.

The journal publishes writers who pay attention to place: essays, dispatches, photographs made with intention. Each issue is organized around a season and a question.

Working on Field Notes taught me that editing is also a kind of writing — a listening for what wants to be said, and a clearing of everything that isn't that.

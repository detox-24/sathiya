March Rain

Woke to the sound of it before I opened my eyes. Rain on the skylight — that specific rhythm that means the day will be slow and permitted.

Made coffee and stood at the window for longer than I meant to. The street was nearly empty. A man walked by with no umbrella, unhurried, face tilted up. I felt oddly moved by this.

There is something I want to say about threshold weather — the kind that lives between seasons, neither one thing nor another. I am always most myself at these edges.

Read until noon. Didn’t answer a single message. Cooked soup from what was already in the kitchen. Called my mother.

This, I think, is what a good day feels like. I want to remember to want this.
